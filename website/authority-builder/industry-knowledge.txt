TERMINOLOGY:
  [1]
    TERM: MAXIMUM MEDICAL IMPROVEMENT (MMI)
    CONTEXT: Medical/Legal Definition
    DEFINITION: The point at which an injured worker's condition has stabilized and is not expected to improve further with additional medical treatment.
  [2]
    CONTEXT: Compensation Structure
    DEFINITION: The legal doctrine that prevents employees from suing their employers for negligence in exchange for guaranteed workers' compensation benefits.
    TERM: EXCLUSIVE REMEDY
  [3]
    CONTEXT: Disability Rating
    DEFINITION: A benefit paid when a worker has a permanent loss of function even after reaching MMI, calculated based on state-mandated impairment schedules.
    TERM: PERMANENT PARTIAL DISABILITY (PPD)
  [4]
    TERM: INDEPENDENT MEDICAL EXAM (IME)
    DEFINITION: A physician selected and paid for by the insurance company to evaluate the claimant, often resulting in conflicting medical opinions.
    CONTEXT: Administrative Procedure
  [5]
    TERM: ARISING OUT OF EMPLOYMENT (AOE/COE)
    DEFINITION: The legal standard determining whether an injury is compensable; it must be related to the duties and location of the job.
    CONTEXT: Legal Criteria
  [6]
    TERM: OCCUPATIONAL DISEASE
    CONTEXT: Occupational Health
    DEFINITION: A condition that results from the nature of the employment (e.g., carpal tunnel, hearing loss, or chemical exposure) rather than a one-time accident.
