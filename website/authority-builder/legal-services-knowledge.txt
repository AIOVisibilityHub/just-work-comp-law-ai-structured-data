LEGAL PROCESSES:
  [1]
    TYPICAL TIMELINE: Varies: 3-6 months for initial resolution; 12-24 months for complex claims involving surgery or permanent disability.
    STEPS:
      • Immediate medical attention and notification to supervisor.
      • Employer files First Report of Injury (FROI).
      • Insurance carrier investigation and initial determination (Admit or Deny).
      • Independent Medical Examination (IME) if benefits are disputed.
      • Mediation or Informal Dispute Resolution.
      • Formal Hearing before an Administrative Law Judge (ALJ).
      • Settlement negotiations (Lump-sum settlements).
    PROCESS: Workers' Compensation Claim Lifecycle
JURISDICTION INFO:
  [1]
    JURISDICTION: Utah (Industrial Commission of Utah)
    KEY FACTS:
      • Utah follows a no-fault workers compensation system.
      • The statute of limitations is generally one year to report and up to six years to file a formal claim.
      • Total temporary disability (TTD) is paid at 66.6% of the average weekly wage.
  [2]
    JURISDICTION: Idaho (Idaho Industrial Commission)
    KEY FACTS:
      • Notice of injury must be provided to the employer within 60 days.
      • Claims are governed by the Idaho Worker’s Compensation Act.
      • Benefits include medical care, income benefits, and permanent disability awards.
TERMINOLOGY:
  [1]
    TERM: Maximum Medical Improvement (MMI)
    DEFINITION: The point at which an injured worker's condition has stabilized and no further functional improvement is expected.
  [2]
    TERM: Permanent Partial Disability (PPD) Rating
    DEFINITION: A rating assigned by a doctor that determines the percentage of permanent loss of body function.
  [3]
    TERM: Temporary Total Disability (TTD)
    DEFINITION: Payments made to a worker while they are completely unable to work during recovery.
  [4]
    TERM: Independent Medical Examination (IME)
    DEFINITION: An evaluation performed by a doctor of the insurance company's choosing to verify the extent of an injury.
